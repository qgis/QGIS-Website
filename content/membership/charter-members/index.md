<h2 class="section-title">What is a Charter Member?</h2>
</div>

Charter Members are individuals who have demonstrated sustained and significant contributions to the OSGeo community. Charter membership is the highest form of individual recognition within OSGeo and comes with important governance responsibilities.

Charter Members are the heart of OSGeo's democratic governance structure. They:

- **Vote** in annual board elections
- **Nominate** candidates for board and charter membership
- **Guide** the foundation's strategic direction
- **Represent** OSGeo at conferences and events
- **Mentor** new community members

<h2 class="section-title">Becoming a Charter Member</h2>
</div>

Charter membership is **earned through contribution**, not purchased or applied for directly. The path to charter membership typically involves years of sustained contribution to the OSGeo community.

### The Nomination Process

1. **Contribute** consistently to the OSGeo community over time
2. **Get nominated** by an existing Charter Member who recognizes your contributions
3. **Vote** by existing Charter Members during the annual membership period
4. **Welcome** to the charter membership upon successful election

Charter Member elections typically take place annually alongside board elections.

<h2 class="section-title">Charter Member Responsibilities</h2>
</div>

### Voting

Charter Members vote in annual board elections, determining the leadership of OSGeo. This is both a right and a responsibility.

### Nominations

Charter Members can nominate:
- Candidates for the OSGeo Board
- New Charter Member candidates
- Recipients for OSGeo awards

### Community Leadership

Charter Members are expected to:
- Continue contributing to the community
- Mentor new community members
- Represent OSGeo professionally
- Participate in governance discussions

<h2 class="section-title">Emeritus Members</h2>
</div>

Charter Members who are no longer actively contributing may be designated as Emeritus Members. Emeritus status recognizes past contributions while keeping the active voting membership current.

Emeritus Members:
- Retain recognition for their contributions
- Can return to active status by resuming contributions
- Are listed separately from active members
