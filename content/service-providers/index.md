## Professional Support

While OSGeo software is free to use, many organizations need professional support for implementation, customization, training, and maintenance. Service providers fill this role, offering commercial services built on OSGeo projects.

<div class="notification is-info is-light">
<strong>Note:</strong> OSGeo does not endorse any specific service provider. This directory is provided as a community resource.
</div>

## Find a Service Provider

Service providers are listed on the OSGeo wiki, organized by:

- **Geographic region** - Find local support
- **Project expertise** - Specialists in specific software
- **Service type** - Development, training, hosting, etc.

[Browse Service Provider Directory](https://wiki.osgeo.org/wiki/Service_Providers){.button .is-osgeo-primary .is-large}

## Become a Listed Service Provider

If your organization provides professional services for OSGeo software, you can add your listing to the wiki directory.

### Requirements

- Provide services related to OSGeo projects
- Have verifiable experience and references
- Agree to accurate representation of services
- Maintain current contact information

[Add Your Listing](https://wiki.osgeo.org/wiki/Service_Providers){.button .is-osgeo-secondary .is-medium}

### Why Use a Service Provider?

- **Expertise** - Deep knowledge of specific software
- **Speed** - Faster implementation
- **Support** - Professional SLAs
- **Training** - Get your team up to speed
- **Custom Development** - Features you need
{{< box-end >}}

{{< box-start classes="has-background-light mt-4" >}}
### Questions to Ask

When evaluating service providers:

1. What projects have you contributed to?
2. Can you provide references?
3. What are your support response times?
4. Do you contribute back to open source?
5. What's your experience with my use case?
{{< box-end >}}
