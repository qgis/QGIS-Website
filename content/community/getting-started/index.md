## Welcome to OSGeo!

Whether you're a GIS professional, developer, researcher, student, or simply curious about open source geospatial technology, there's a place for you in the OSGeo community.

This guide will help you take your first steps into the world of open source geospatial software.

## Step 2: Learn

Once you've chosen software to try, these resources will help you learn:

### Documentation
Every OSGeo project has documentation. Start with the official docs for your chosen software.

### Tutorials
- Project-specific tutorials on YouTube
- [QGIS Training Manual](https://docs.qgis.org/latest/en/docs/training_manual/)
- Community-created guides and courses

### Workshops
- [FOSS4G conferences](/initiatives/foss4g/) offer hands-on workshops
- Local user groups often host training sessions
- [Geo for All](/initiatives/geo-for-all/) provides educational resources

## Step 3: Connect with the Community

The OSGeo community is welcoming and helpful. Here's how to connect:

### Mailing Lists
Join project mailing lists to ask questions, share knowledge, and stay informed.

[Browse Mailing Lists](/community/mailing-lists/){.button .is-osgeo-secondary .is-medium}

### Chat
Many projects have real-time chat channels for quick questions:
- IRC channels on Libera.Chat
- Matrix rooms
- Project-specific Discord/Slack

### Local Chapters
Find geospatial enthusiasts in your area through [Local Chapters](/local-chapters/).

### Social Media
Follow [@osgeo](https://fosstodon.org/@osgeo) and project accounts for news and updates.
