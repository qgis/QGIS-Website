### FOSS4G Conference

The cornerstone event for the open geospatial community. Since 2004, FOSS4G has brought together 1,000+ participants annually for workshops, presentations, and community building.

**FOSS4G 2025:** Auckland, New Zealand
**Dates:** November 17-23, 2025

### Geo for All

Making geospatial education accessible to everyone. A global network of 100+ labs partnering with universities, institutions, and industry to expand open source geospatial education and research.

**Partnerships with:** ICA, ISPRS, UN-GIS, AGILE, UCGIS

[Explore Geo for All &rarr;](/initiatives/geo-for-all/)

### Google Summer of Code

Mentoring the next generation of open source developers. Since 2007, OSGeo has graduated **180+ students** through GSoC, introducing them to real-world software development.

**2023:** 7 students graduated
**2022:** 12 students graduated

[GSoC with OSGeo &rarr;](/initiatives/gsoc/)

### OSGeoLive

A self-contained bootable environment with 50+ pre-configured open source geospatial applications. Perfect for evaluation, training, and outreach - no installation required.

- Sample datasets included
- Quick-start documentation
- Available as DVD, USB, or VM
- Based on Lubuntu

[Get OSGeoLive &rarr;](/initiatives/osgeolive/)

<h2 class="section-title">Start an Initiative</h2>
</div>

Have an idea for a new OSGeo initiative? We welcome proposals from the community. Successful initiatives align with OSGeo's mission, have clear goals, and engage the community.

{{< columns-start >}}

{{< column-start class="is-8" >}}

### How to Propose an Initiative

1. **Discuss** your idea on the OSGeo discuss mailing list
2. **Draft** a proposal outlining goals, resources needed, and community benefit
3. **Present** to the board or relevant committee
4. **Build** support from community members
5. **Launch** with OSGeo backing and resources
